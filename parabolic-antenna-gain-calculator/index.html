Antenna Diameter & Efficiency

Explanation & Formulas

Parabolic Antenna Gain (dBi):

Gain = 10 × log₁₀ [ η × (π × D / λ)² ]

where:

  • η = aperture efficiency
  • D = dish diameter (meters)
  • λ = wavelength

This is the theoretical maximum gain for a given physical aperture and efficiency. Real-world gain can be lower due to surface roughness, feed spillover, blockage, etc.

Typical Efficiencies

  • 35% – Poor feed / electrically small dishes
  • 50% – Average amateur tropo setup
  • 60% – Reasonably well-illuminated dish (common reference)
  • 70–75% – High-performance optimized systems (EME, radio astronomy)

Note: Gain increases with frequency (smaller λ) and with diameter. Efficiency has a strong impact — going from 50% to 70% gives almost +1.5 dB extra gain.
