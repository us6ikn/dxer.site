About EME Observer

EME Observer is a tool for visualizing Earth-Moon-Earth (EME) signal activity, providing real-time insights into propagation, signal paths, and observation data. It is designed for amateur radio operators and enthusiasts who are active on 10GHz moonbounce operations.

Data and Accuracy

The system relies on crowdsourced observation data, satellite prediction models, and propagation calculations. While designed for high accuracy, some variance may occur depending on data freshness, station reports, and propagation irregularities.

Quick Start

Map View

The map shows current EME signal detections and observer locations. Clicking a station icon reveals signal details and time of last detection.

Table View

The table lists all recent EME contacts, with options to sort or filter by time, callsign, or signal strength.

Statistics

View DXCC totals, country-based detection charts, and trends over time. Hover over a bar to see the station count per country.
