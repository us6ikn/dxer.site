EME Moon Ephemeris: Yearly Overview 2025-2040

Continuation of the classic F1EHN graphical format – recreated and maintained by SA5IKN

The annual EME Moon ephemeris overview has become an essential planning tool for EME operators across all bands. It provides a holistic, single-page view of the entire year, bringing together the key moon dynamics parameters used in typical EME QSO planning - moon declination, excess loss due to varying distance, moon-sun separation, and moon-Sgr A separation - all in one place.

This unified perspective complements conventional EME planners by presenting the entire year on a single (printable) page, enabling operators to identify optimal dates for contests, activity weekends, and the best operating opportunities with a single glance.

The familiar graphical style was originally introduced by Franck F5SE in 2005 and later significantly refined by Jean-Jacques F1EHN following Franck’s passing in 2017. Through all these years the annual ephemeris has been a trusted resource within the EME community, regularly appearing in 432 MHz and Above EME Newsletter, Radio REF Magazine, and DUBUS.

Following the sad passing of Jean-Jacques F1EHN, Peter G3LTF, editor of the 432 MHz and Above EME Newsletter, began searching for someone to continue producing the yearly ephemeris in the established format. I took on this role in September 2025.

As Jean-Jacques’ original code was no longer available, I rebuilt the ephemeris generation tools from the ground up, aiming to preserve the familiar look and clarity of his design, both as a tribute and because it remains highly valued by the community.

With prior experience developing a new EME planner, EME Observer, as well as my own microwave EME antenna tracker, and a background in theoretical physics (PhD), I found the process both enjoyable and rewarding. I am pleased that the new overview closely matches Jean-Jacques’ original design, helping ensure that his legacy continues.
