Antenna Diameter & Efficiency

Explanation & Formulas

Parabolic Dish Gain (dBi):

Gain (dBi) = 10 × log₁₀ [ η × (π × D / λ)² ]

where:

  • η = aperture efficiency
  • D = dish diameter (meters)
  • λ = wavelength

This is the theoretical maximum gain for a given physical aperture and efficiency. Real-world gain can be slightly lower due to surface roughness, feed spillover, blockage, etc.

Typical Efficiencies

  • 35% – Poor feed / small dishes
  • 50% – Average amateur setup
  • 60% – Good illuminated dish (common reference)
  • 70–75% – High-performance optimized systems (high-end EME, radio astronomy)

Note: Gain increases with frequency (smaller λ) and with larger diameter. Efficiency has a strong impact — going from 50% to 70% gives almost +1.5 dB extra gain.
