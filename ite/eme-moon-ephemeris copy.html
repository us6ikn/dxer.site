The yearly EME Moon ephemeris overview has become an essential planning tool for many operators active on VHF, UHF and microwave EME. For more than two decades, its distinctive visual format has allowed quick identification of declination trends, perigee/apogee cycles, libration extremes and other parameters critical to successful Moonbounce activity.

Background and continuity

The ephemeris overview in its familiar graphical style was originally introduced by Franck F5SE, and later further developed and refined by Jean-Jacques F1EHN. Jean-Jacques‚Äô versions became widely used within the EME community and were regularly published via the 432 MHz and Above EME Newsletter edited by Peter G3LTF.

After the very sad passing of Jean-Jacques, Peter began looking for someone to continue producing the ephemeris in a compatible format so that this valuable resource would not disappear. In September 2025, I took on this responsibility.

Recreating the ephemeris tools

Jean-Jacques‚Äô original software was unfortunately no longer available. This meant the charts had to be recreated from scratch, with the goal of preserving the familiar appearance and readability that operators had come to rely on. This was done both as a tribute to Jean-Jacques‚Äô work and because the design itself is genuinely effective.

The development built on my earlier experience creating the EME Observer, designing my own microwave EME antenna tracking system, and my academic background in theoretical physics. Rebuilding the ephemeris generator has been a technically enjoyable project, and I am pleased that the current results closely match the established format while being generated by entirely new software.

Looking forward

Beyond simply maintaining continuity, the long-term goal is to ensure that these tools remain available, accurate and adaptable. The new software foundation makes it possible to extend functionality in the future, including improved interactivity, custom visualisation options, and integration with other EME planning tools, while preserving the familiar format trusted by the community.
