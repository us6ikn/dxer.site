Annual EME Moon Ephemeris 2025–2040

Continuation of the classic F1EHN graphical format of the Moon ephemeris overview

The yearly EME Moon ephemeris overview has become an essential planning tool for operators active on VHF, UHF and microwave bands. For more than two decades, its distinctive visual format has enabled quick identification of declination trends, perigee/apogee cycles, libration extremes and other parameters critical to successful Moonbounce activity.

Background and Continuity

The familiar graphical style was originally introduced by Franck F5SE and significantly refined by Jean-Jacques F1EHN. Jean-Jacques’ versions gained widespread adoption in the EME community and were regularly featured in the 432 MHz and Above EME Newsletter edited by Peter G3LTF.

Following Jean-Jacques' passing, Peter G3LTF sought someone to continue this valuable resource in a compatible format. In September 2025, I accepted this responsibility to ensure continuity.

Recreating the Ephemeris Tools

The original software by F1EHN was unfortunately no longer available. This required rebuilding the charts from scratch while preserving the appearance and readability that the community has relied on for many years.

The project drew on my previous work with the EME Observer, experience designing microwave EME antenna tracking systems, and background in theoretical physics. The result closely matches the established format while being generated by entirely new, modern code.

Looking Forward

Beyond maintaining continuity, the long-term goal is to keep this resource accurate, accessible and adaptable. The new software foundation opens possibilities for future enhancements: better interactivity, custom visualisation options, and integration with other EME planning tools – all while preserving the format the community trusts.
